The Rivalry

South Africa and New Zealand have been fighting for the top spot in rugby for over 100 years, with their intense rivalry beginning in 1921. This rivalry is widely considered the fiercest in rugby history, often described as a "war" or a "100-year battle".

Story

Though New Zealand held the top spot for a while not too long ago, South Africa were still the Championchip trophy holders.

This is due to how despite losing the 2023 World Cup, New Zealand's consistently high performance in other tests (like winning the 2023 Rugby Championship earlier) kept their ranking points extremely high, sometimes allowing them to stay at #1 or return to it briefly even when South Africa was the reigning world champion.

Roles

Centre — Runs the ball in the midfield, makes tackles, and creates opportunities for the wingers.

Fullback — Last line of defence, catches kicks, and joins attacks when needed.

Prop — Front row of the scrum, does the heavy lifting and physicality up front.

Loose Forward — Athletic forwards who carry the ball, make tackles, and link play between forwards and backs.

Hooker — Middle of the front row, hooks the ball in scrums and throws in at lineouts.

Wing — Wide players on the edge, usually the fastest on the team and finishes tries.

Scrumhalf — The link between forwards and backs, feeds the scrum and distributes the ball quickly.

Lock — Tall players in the second row of the scrum, win lineouts and provide power.

Flyhalf — The playmaker, controls the game, kicks, passes, and decides how attacks are run.
